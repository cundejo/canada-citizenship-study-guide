# Chapter 7: The Justice System

## Core Principles

- **Presumption of innocence** — You are innocent until proven guilty
- **Due process** — Everyone has the right to a fair trial and legal representation
- **Rule of law** — Everyone is subject to the law, including police and government

---

## The Courts

- **Supreme Court of Canada** — The **highest court** in Canada; final say on all legal matters
- Courts in Canada help ensure laws are applied fairly
- Canada's legal system comes from **English common law** and (in Quebec) **French civil law**
- **You are allowed to question the police** about their service or conduct

---

## Police Services

| Level | Force |
|-------|-------|
| **National** | **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** — Serve across Canada |
| **Provincial** | Ontario Provincial Police (OPP), Sûreté du Québec (SQ) |
| **Municipal** | Local city police forces |

- The RCMP serve as the police in **all provinces and territories except Ontario and Quebec** (which have their own provincial forces)
- The RCMP is also known as the "Mounties" and are a symbol of Canada

---

## Legal Aid

- If you cannot afford a lawyer, you can apply for **legal aid** to get legal help
