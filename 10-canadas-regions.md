# Chapter 10: Canada's Regions

## Overview

- Canada is the **second-largest country on Earth** (~10 million km²)
- **Three oceans** border Canada: Pacific (west), Atlantic (east), Arctic (north)
- **10 provinces** and **3 territories**
- Population: approximately **34 million** people
- **Ottawa** is the national capital (chosen by Queen Victoria in **1857**)

---

## Five Regions at a Glance

| Region | Provinces/Territories |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Atlantic** | Newfoundland & Labrador, PEI, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick |
| **Central Canada** | Quebec, Ontario |
| **Prairie Provinces** | Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Alberta |
| **West Coast** | British Columbia |
| **Northern Territories** | Yukon, Northwest Territories, Nunavut |

---

## Provinces, Capitals & Key Facts

### Atlantic Provinces

| Province | Capital | Key Facts |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Newfoundland & Labrador** | St. John's | Most easterly point in North America; fisheries; offshore oil; oldest British colony |
| **Prince Edward Island** | Charlottetown | Smallest province; potatoes; birthplace of Confederation; Confederation Bridge; Anne of Green Gables |
| **Nova Scotia** | Halifax | Most populous Atlantic province; world's highest tides (Bay of Fundy); shipbuilding; largest east coast port; largest naval base |
| **New Brunswick** | Fredericton | Only **officially bilingual province**; ~1/3 population Francophone; St. John River; founded by Loyalists |

### Central Canada

| Province | Capital | Key Facts |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Quebec** | Québec City | ~8 million people; 3/4 speak French; largest producer of pulp/paper and hydro-electricity; Montreal is 2nd largest mainly French-speaking city in the world (after Paris); pharmaceuticals and aeronautics |
| **Ontario** | Toronto | 12+ million people (1/3 of Canada); Toronto is the largest city and main financial centre; Niagara region; Five Great Lakes; largest French-speaking population outside Quebec |

### Prairie Provinces

| Province | Capital | Key Facts |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Manitoba** | Winnipeg | Agriculture, mining, hydro-electric; 14% Ukrainian origins; largest Aboriginal population of any province (15%+); Portage and Main intersection |
| **Saskatchewan** | Regina | 40% of arable land in Canada; largest producer of grains and oilseeds; world's richest uranium and potash deposits; RCMP training academy in Regina |
| **Alberta** | Edmonton | Most populous Prairie province; largest producer of oil and gas; oil sands; Banff National Park (est. 1885); Lake Louise; Rocky Mountains; cattle ranches; dinosaur fossils in Badlands |

### West Coast

| Province | Capital | Key Facts |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **British Columbia** | Victoria | 4 million people; Pacific gateway; Port of Vancouver (largest/busiest port); forestry (most valuable in Canada); Okanagan Valley wines; ~600 provincial parks; most extensive park system; Chinese and Punjabi are most spoken languages after English |

### Northern Territories

| Territory | Capital | Key Facts |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Yukon** | Whitehorse | Gold Rush of 1890s; coldest temperature in Canada (−63°C); Mount Logan (highest mountain in Canada) |
| **Northwest Territories** | Yellowknife | "Diamond capital of North America"; Mackenzie River (2nd longest river system in North America, 4,200 km); 50%+ Aboriginal population |
| **Nunavut** | Iqaluit | Established **1999**; means "our land" in Inuktitut; 85% Inuit; legislature chooses premier by consensus |

- The North contains **one-third** of Canada's land mass but only ~100,000 people
- Known as the "Land of the Midnight Sun" — up to 24 hours of daylight in summer

---

## Mount Logan

- Located in the **Yukon**; **highest mountain in Canada**
- Named after **Sir William Logan**, a famous geologist who founded the Geological Survey of Canada (1842–1869)
