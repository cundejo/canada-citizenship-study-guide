# Chapter 6: Federal Elections

## Voting Basics

- **Who can vote?** — Canadian citizens aged **18 or older**
- **Secret ballot** — Nobody can watch you vote, and nobody can force you to reveal your choice
- **You are NOT obliged** to tell anyone how you voted
- Elections held every **4 years** (third Monday in October), but can be called sooner
- Canada has **308 electoral districts** (also called **ridings** or **constituencies**)

---

## How Voting Works

1. You are placed on the **voters' list** (or register at the poll)
2. Go to your assigned **polling station** on election day
3. Go behind a **screen** and mark your ballot in secret
4. Your vote is counted — the candidate with the **most votes** in your riding wins

---

## Types of Government

| Type | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Majority government** | The governing party holds **more than half** the seats in the House of Commons |
| **Minority government** | The governing party holds the most seats, but **less than half** |
| **Coalition** | Two or more parties joining together to form a majority |

---

## After the Election

- The **party with the most seats** forms the government
- The leader of that party becomes the **Prime Minister**
- The **Official Opposition** is the party with the second-most seats
- The PM selects **Cabinet ministers** from elected members of Parliament (and sometimes Senators)

---

## Three Levels of Government

| Level | Responsibilities |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Federal** | Defence, foreign policy, citizenship, criminal law, banking, Indigenous affairs |
| **Provincial/Territorial** | Education, health care, highways, natural resources, policing |
| **Municipal (local)** | Snow removal, recycling, firefighting, local police, transit, libraries |

- Municipal governments are led by a **mayor** (or reeve) and **councillors**
- They pass **by-laws** (local regulations)

---

## Major Federal Parties (at time of guide publication)

- **Conservative Party** — Centre-right
- **New Democratic Party (NDP)** — Centre-left
- **Liberal Party** — Centre
