# Chapter 1: Rights and Responsibilities of Citizenship

## The Oath of Citizenship

- When you become a citizen, you swear an **oath of allegiance to the Sovereign** (the King or Queen of Canada)
- You also promise to **observe the laws of Canada** and **fulfill your duties as a Canadian citizen**

---

## Key Documents

- **Magna Carta (1215)** — Also known as the Great Charter of Freedoms; signed in England; foundation of many rights
- **Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms (1982)** — Part of the Constitution; the highest law of Canada
- **Habeas corpus** — The right not to be held without a fair trial; comes from English common law

---

## Rights of Canadian Citizens

- **Mobility rights** — The right to live and work anywhere in Canada
- **Aboriginal peoples' rights** — Original rights recognized in the Constitution
- **Official language rights** — Right to federal government services in English or French
- **Multiculturalism** — A fundamental characteristic of Canadian identity and heritage
- **Equality of men and women** — Guaranteed in Canadian law

### Four Fundamental Freedoms

1. **Freedom of conscience and religion**
2. **Freedom of thought, belief, opinion, and expression** (including freedom of the press)
3. **Freedom of peaceful assembly**
4. **Freedom of association**

---

## Responsibilities of Canadian Citizens

- **Obeying the law** — One of Canada's founding principles is the rule of law
- **Taking responsibility for oneself and one's family**
- **Serving on a jury** — A duty when called
- **Voting in elections** — A responsibility of citizenship
- **Helping others in the community** — Volunteering
- **Protecting and enjoying our heritage and environment**
- **Defending Canada** — There is no compulsory military service, but helping to defend Canada is expected if needed

---

## Things Banned in Canada

- **Barbaric cultural practices** — Including spousal abuse, "honour killings," female genital mutilation, forced marriage, or other gender-based violence
- **Discrimination** — Based on race, sex, religion, sexual orientation, or disability
