# Chapter 2: Who We Are

## Three Founding Peoples

1. **Aboriginal (Indigenous) peoples** — The first inhabitants
2. **French** — Early settlers and explorers
3. **British** — Colonists and Loyalists

---

## Aboriginal Peoples

Three main groups:

| Group | % of Aboriginal Population | Key Facts |
|-------|---------------------------|-----------|
| **First Nations** | ~65% | Diverse nations across Canada |
| **Métis** | ~30% | Mixed Indigenous and European ancestry; distinct culture |
| **Inuit** | ~4% | "Inuit" means "the people" in Inuktitut; live mainly in the Arctic |

- **Royal Proclamation of 1763** — Recognized Aboriginal rights; called the "Indian Magna Carta"
- **Residential schools** — Government policy that harmed Indigenous communities; **PM apologized in 2008**
- About 65% of Aboriginal people are First Nations, 30% Métis, 4% Inuit

---

## English and French

- **English and French are the two official languages** of Canada
- You must have adequate knowledge of English or French to become a citizen (unless 55+)
- **~18 million Anglophones** (English speakers) and **~7 million Francophones** (French speakers)
- **New Brunswick** is the only **officially bilingual province**
- **Quebecers** — Recognized as a nation within a united Canada (House of Commons motion, 2006)

### The Acadians
- French settlers who began arriving in **1604**
- **Great Upheaval (1755–1763)** — British deported about two-thirds of Acadians from the Maritimes
- Today about 1 million Acadians live mainly in the Maritime provinces and Quebec

---

## Official Languages Act (1969)

Three main objectives:
1. Establish equality between French and English in Parliament and government institutions
2. Maintain and develop official language minority communities
3. Promote equality of French and English in Canadian society

---

## Diversity and Immigration

- Canada is one of the most diverse and multicultural countries in the world
- Largest ethnic/cultural groups: English, French, Scottish, Irish, German, Italian, Chinese, Aboriginal, Ukrainian, Dutch, South Asian, Scandinavian
- Since 1970, most immigrants come from **Asia**
- Two-thirds of immigrants become **Canadian citizens**
