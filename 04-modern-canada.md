# Chapter 4: Modern Canada

## International Engagement

- Canada joined **NATO (1949)** — North Atlantic Treaty Organization; collective defense
- **NORAD** — North American Aerospace Defence Command (joint with the USA)
- **United Nations (UN)** — Canada is a founding member
- **Korean War (1950–1953)** — 26,000+ Canadians served; approximately **500 died**
- Canada has been active in **UN peacekeeping** missions worldwide
- **La Francophonie (1970)** — Organization of French-speaking nations; Canada is a key member

---

## Quebec and Sovereignty

- **Quiet Revolution (1960s)** — Period of rapid change in Quebec; secularization, modernization, and growth of Quebec nationalism
- **Official Languages Act (1969)** — Made English and French equal in all federal institutions
- **Quebec sovereignty referendums:**
  - **1980** — "No" to sovereignty won (~60%)
  - **1995** — "No" to sovereignty won by a very narrow margin (~50.6%)

## Arts and Culture

- **Group of Seven** — Famous Canadian painters who depicted the Canadian landscape
- **Emily Carr** — Renowned painter of the West Coast and Indigenous themes
- Canadians have made major contributions to music, literature, film, and visual arts

## Sports

| Sport | Key Facts |
|-------|-----------|
| **Hockey** | National winter sport; most popular spectator sport; developed in Canada in the 1800s |
| **Lacrosse** | Official national summer sport; first played by Aboriginal peoples |
| **Canadian football** | Second most popular sport |
| **Basketball** | Invented by Canadian **James Naismith** in **1891** |
| **Curling** | Introduced by Scottish pioneers |
| **Soccer** | Most registered players of any sport |

- **Stanley Cup** — Donated by **Lord Stanley**, the Governor General, in **1892**
- **Clarkson Cup** — Established 2005 by Adrienne Clarkson (26th Governor General, first of Asian origin); awarded for women's hockey

## Famous Canadians (Terry Fox)

- **Terry Fox** — In 1980, after losing his leg to cancer, ran the **Marathon of Hope** across Canada to raise money for cancer research
- Ran 5,373 km over 143 days before cancer forced him to stop
- Remains one of Canada's greatest heroes
