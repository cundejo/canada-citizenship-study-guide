# Chapter 5: How Canadians Govern Themselves

## Canada's System of Government — Three Key Features

1. **Federal state** — Powers shared between federal and provincial/territorial governments
2. **Parliamentary democracy** — Elected representatives make laws
3. **Constitutional monarchy** — The Sovereign (King/Queen) is the Head of State

---

## The Three Branches of Government

| Branch | Role | Key People |
|--------|------|------------|
| **Executive** | Carries out government decisions | PM, Cabinet, Governor General |
| **Legislative** | Makes and passes laws | Parliament (Senate + House of Commons) |
| **Judicial** | Interprets and applies the law | Courts (Supreme Court is the highest) |

---

## Parliament — Three Parts

1. **The Sovereign** (represented by the Governor General)
2. **The Senate** (Upper House) — Members are **appointed** (serve until age **75**)
3. **The House of Commons** (Lower House) — Members are **elected** (308 seats/ridings)

---

## Key Roles

| Title | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Head of State** | The Sovereign (King/Queen of Canada) |
| **Governor General** | Represents the Sovereign; appointed (usually for **5 years**) |
| **Head of Government** | The **Prime Minister (PM)** |
| **PM** | Leader of the party with the most seats; selects the **Cabinet** |
| **Cabinet ministers** | Run federal government departments; responsible to Parliament |
| **Opposition** | Parties that are not in government; the largest is the **Official Opposition** |

---

## How a Bill Becomes Law (7 Steps)

1. First reading — Bill is introduced
2. Second reading — Debate on the principle
3. Committee stage — Detailed study, witnesses, amendments
4. Report stage — Report back to the House
5. Third reading — Final debate and vote
6. Senate review — Senate studies, debates, and votes
7. **Royal Assent** — Governor General signs the bill into law

---

## Provincial and Territorial Government

| Level | Leader | Representative of Sovereign |
|-------|--------|-----------------------------|
| **Federal** | Prime Minister | Governor General |
| **Provincial** | **Premier** | **Lieutenant Governor** |
| **Territorial** | Premier | **Commissioner** |

- Each province has its own elected **Legislative Assembly**
- Territories have fewer powers than provinces; governed under federal authority

---

## What "Constitutional Monarchy" Means

- Canada's Head of State is the Sovereign (currently the King)
- The Sovereign reigns but does not govern — the PM and Cabinet govern
- The constitution limits government power and protects citizens' rights
