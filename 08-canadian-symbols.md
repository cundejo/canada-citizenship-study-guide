# Chapter 8: Canadian Symbols

## National Symbols

| Symbol | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **The Crown** | Symbol of government for over 400 years |
| **Canadian flag** | Red-white-red with a maple leaf; adopted in **1965** |
| **Maple leaf** | National symbol since the 1700s; adopted by French Canadians |
| **Fleur-de-lys** | Symbol on the Quebec flag; represents French heritage |
| **Coat of arms** | Features the motto **"A Mari Usque Ad Mare"** — "From sea to sea" |
| **Parliament Buildings** | Gothic Revival style; located on Parliament Hill, Ottawa |
| **Peace Tower** | Central tower of Parliament; completed **1927**; houses the **Books of Remembrance** (honoring Canadians who died in wars) |
| **The beaver** | National animal; symbol since Hudson's Bay Company days; appears on the five-cent coin |
| **RCMP** | The "Mounties"; iconic red-serge uniform; symbol of Canada worldwide |

---

## National Anthem — "O Canada"

- Proclaimed the national anthem in **1980**
- First sung in **Quebec City in 1880**
- French and English Canadians sing different words

---

## Royal Anthem

- "God Save the Queen (or King)" — Played when honouring the Sovereign

---

## The Order of Canada and Honours

- **Order of Canada** — Canada's own honours system, established **1967** (centennial of Confederation)
- **Victoria Cross (V.C.)** — The **highest honour** available to Canadians; awarded for conspicuous bravery
  - **96 Canadians** have received the V.C. since 1854
  - First Canadian recipient: **Alexander Roberts Dunn** (1854, Charge of the Light Brigade)

## Remembrance Day (November 11)

- Canadians wear the **red poppy** to remember those who served and died in wars
- "In Flanders Fields" — Famous poem by Canadian **Lt. Col. John McCrae**
