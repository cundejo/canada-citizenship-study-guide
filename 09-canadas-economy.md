# Chapter 9: Canada's Economy

## Overview

- Canada has one of the **top ten largest economies** in the world
- Member of the **G8** (leading industrialized countries: USA, UK, Germany, France, Italy, Japan, Russia, Canada)
- Commerce and trade are the engine of economic growth

---

## Trade

- **Largest trading partner:** The United States
- Over **three-quarters** of Canadian exports go to the U.S.
- Canada-U.S. have the **biggest bilateral trading relationship** in the world
- **Free trade with the U.S.** enacted in **1988**
- **NAFTA (1994)** — North American Free Trade Agreement (Canada, U.S., Mexico); ~444 million people, $1+ trillion in trade
- Canada exports energy products, industrial goods, machinery, automotive, agricultural, fishing, and forestry products
- The Canada-U.S. border is traditionally known as "the world's longest undefended border"

---

## Three Main Types of Industries

| Type | Details | % of Workers |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Service industries** | Transportation, education, health care, construction, banking, retail, tourism, government | **75%+** |
| **Manufacturing** | Paper, tech equipment, aerospace, automobiles, machinery, food, clothing | — |
| **Natural resources** | Forestry, fishing, agriculture, mining, energy | — |

---

## Key Economic Facts

- Service industries employ more than **75%** of working Canadians
- Natural resources have played a major role in Canada's history and development
- A large percentage of exports are natural resources commodities
- Oil discovered in **Alberta in 1947** fueled major economic growth
